1. Patient Entry & Registration
Arrival: Patients often walk in without appointments (walk-in model dominates).

Registration Desk:

Patient details recorded in a paper register (name, age, sex, address, sometimes NHIS number).

Returning patients may bring a folder/card kept at the facility or at home.

Queue numbers are issued manually.

Pain point: Duplicate records are common; identifiers are inconsistent.

Takes history, performs exam, writes diagnosis and treatment plan.

4. Laboratory / Diagnostics
Lab request form: Handwritten slip carried by patient to lab.

Lab staff: Perform test, record results in lab register, return slip to patient.

Result delivery: Patient carries result back to clinician.

Pain point: Results can be lost, delayed, or misfiled.

5. Pharmacy / Dispensing
Prescription slip: Patient presents to pharmacy.

Pharmacist/technician: Dispenses drugs, records in dispensing register.

Stock management: Often manual; stock-outs common.

Pain point: No real-time link between prescribing and stock availability.

6. Billing / Insurance (NHIS)
NHIS (National Health Insurance Scheme):

Claims require detailed paper forms and facility registers.

Staff often re-enter data into NHIS claim software.

Private pay: Cash collected at cashier, receipt issued.

Pain point: Double entry, delays in NHIS reimbursement.

7. Reporting & Administration
Monthly reporting:

Facility staff aggregate data from registers (OPD visits, deliveries, immunizations, etc.).

Reports submitted to District Health Information System (DHIS2).

Pain point: Manual tallying is error-prone and time-consuming.
